Artemis II

Humanity returned to the Moon — April 1–10, 2026

On April 1, 2026, NASA's Artemis II mission launched four astronauts — Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen — on a 10-day journey around the Moon aboard the Orion spacecraft. It was the first crewed lunar mission since Apollo 17 in 1972. The crew flew a free-return trajectory, capturing breathtaking photos of Earth, the Moon, and even a rare in-space solar eclipse. They splashed down safely in the Pacific Ocean on April 10.
